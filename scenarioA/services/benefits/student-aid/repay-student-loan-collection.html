Having trouble making a payment

If you are having trouble making a payment, you can first try to reduce the amount you pay each month.

  • Reduce your monthly payment

You can also look into the types of repayment assistance we offer:

  • Get repayment assistance

Missing a payment will have a negative impact on your credit rating.

Contact us to discuss options

Whether you already missed a loan payment or not, contact us to discuss options to help you get back on track:

  • contact the National Student Loans Service Centre (NSLC)
  • if you live in Alberta, Manitoba, Nova Scotia or Prince Edward Island, also contact your provincial loan provider

Getting your loan out of collection

If you missed payments on your Canada Student Loan for 270 days or more (9 months), your loan becomes in default. It is sent to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) for collection.

Canada Student Loan rehabilitation is an opportunity to have your loan taken out of collection. The loan would then be returned to the NSLSC.

  • Contact the NSLC to find out if your loan can be rehabilitated

Note: If you have a judgment or legal action on your account, you may not be eligible for Canada Student Loan rehabilitation. Contact the NSLSC and ask to speak with a Canada Student Loans Program (CSLP) client relations officer to discuss your situation.

For the provincial portion of your student loan, contact the student loan provider in your province of permanent residence.

Rehabilitate your Canada Student Loan

If you are eligible for Canada Student Loan rehabilitation, you must contact the NSLSC to:

  • arrange a repayment schedule with the CRA
  • repay all interest owed on your Canada Student Loan
  • make the equivalent of 2 monthly payments on the loan. You need to pay the agreed monthly payment that is set out in your repayment schedule with CRA. CRA may also allow you to make a lump sum payment combining the two monthly payments and the interest owed.

After you make your payments, contact the NSLSC. Ask to speak with a CSLP client relations officer to inform them that you did the necessary payments to rehabilitate your loan.

Contact NSLSC

If you have not received a decision letter from the CSLP within 20 business days, call the NSLSC and ask to speak with a CSLP client relations officer about your loan rehabilitation.
