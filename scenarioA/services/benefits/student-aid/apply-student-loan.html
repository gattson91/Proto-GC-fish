Apply for student grants and loans - What you could get

Grants and loans for full-time and part-time students

The Government of Canada offers student grants and loans to full-time and part-time students. Grants and loans help students pay for their post-secondary education.

  • Apply for grants and loans in one application, directly with your province of residence.
  • Loans need to be repayed, with interest after you finish school. Grants do not need to be repayed.
  • You may be eligible for more than 1 type of grant. When you apply with your province, they will assess your eligibility for all grants on offer.

How much can you get

The amount you can receive depends on several factors, including:

  • your province or territory of residence
  • your family income
  • if you have dependants
  • your tuition fees and living expenses
  • if you have a disability

The amount you can receive in grants and loans is calculated when you apply with your province.

Special circumstances

There are other grants you can receive in special circumstances. The amount you can receive is calculated automatically when you apply for student grants and loans at your province:

  • Full-time and part-time students with dependants
  • Students with permanent disabilities
  • Services and Equipment for Students with Permanent Disabilities

There also are special programs and initiatives for Indigenous peoples.

  • Education, training and jobs for Indigenous peoples

Maximum lifetime limit for student aid

There are lifetime limits on the amount of student aid you can receive. This includes both loans and interest-free periods for each loan. Once a lifetime limit has been reached, interest starts to accumulate. You will also have to start paying back the loan six months after you graduate or finish your studies.

Lifetime limits for Canada Student Loans vary:

  • Full-time students who received loans on or after August 1, 1995, can receive student aid for no more than 340 weeks.
  • Full-time students enrolled in doctoral studies can receive student aid for no more than 400 weeks.
  • Students with permanent disability and students who received Canada Student Loans before August 1, 1995, can receive student aid for no more than 520 weeks.
