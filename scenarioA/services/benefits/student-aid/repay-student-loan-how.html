Provincial payments

If you live in Alberta, Manitoba, Nova Scotia or Prince Edward Island

You have provincial and federal parts of your loan that need to be repaid separately.

Follow the instructions from your provincial loan provider to repay your loan. You can follow the instructions on this page for the federal part of your loan.

If you live in Quebec, Nunavut or the Northwest Territories

You pay back your loan directly to your province or territory. Contact your provincial loan provider to arrange payments.

Otherwise

Canada Student Loans from Ontario, Saskatchewan, (others????) are fully repayable through the National Student Loan Service Centre. See more isntructions on this page.

Making your first monthly payment

Six months after you finish school, you will need to start making monthly loan payments. You should receive a repayment plan in the mail from National Student Loan Service Centre (NSLSC) before the six-month period is over.

If you wish to make changes to your payment date, fill out the form and return it. If you do not sign it and send it back, the NSLSC will automatically use the suggested payment plan.

Interest rate options

By default, your student loan has a floating interest rate of:

  • the prime lending rate + 2.5%

The prime lending rate is calculated using the interest rates declared by the five largest Canadian financial institutions as their prime rate. The Canada Student Loans Program discards both the highest and the lowest of those five rates and uses the average of the remaining three.

At any time, after you enter repayment, you can change to a fixed interest rate of:

  • the prime lending rate (at the time you request to change to a fixed rate) + 5%

If you switch to a fixed interest rate, you will not be able to change back to a floating rate.

To choose a fixed interest rate in your repayment plan or to switch to a fixed interest rate anytime during your repayment period, contact us.

  • Use the Repayment estimator to understand the effect of interest rates on your monthly payments.

If you have a provincial part to your loan, it might be under a different interest rate, set by your province.

Pre-authorized monthly payments

Pre-authorized monthly payments will be taken out of your account automatically. You can use the same bank account that your student loans were deposited into to make payments.

If you do not have an authorized bank account on your file, you can sign up for pre-authorized payments:

  • Pre-Authorized Payment form (PDF, 128 KB)

Fill out the form and mail it to:

Change your monthly payment

Change the payment amount

Increasing or decreasing your monthly payment - Above the minimum amount

The amount you pay over and above your minimum monthly payment is applied directly toward the principal amount of your loan. This decreases the total loan amount and the total interest you will have to pay.

  • Customize your monthly payment amount in your NSLSC account

If you increased your monthly payment above the minimum amount, you can decrease it back through your NSLSC account.You cannot reduce it below the minimum amount, unless you do a Revision of Terms.

Reducing your monthly payment below the minimum amount

You can ask to have the terms of your loan changed if you need to reduce your payments below the minimum amount.

With a Revision of Terms, you can to extend your repayment period from 114 months (9.5 years) to up to 174 months (14.5 years).

This will make your monthly payments smaller. However, you'll end up paying more interest in the end because it will take you longer to pay back your loan.

  • Contact the National Student Loans Service Centre (NSLSC) to find out if you can do a Revision of Terms

If you need more help in repaying your loan, go to Repayment assistance.

Lump sum payments

You can make lump sum payments at any time to reduce your total balance. This is one of the fastest ways to pay off your debt.

Payments you make while attending school or during the six month non-repayment period will be applied directly to the principal of your loan. Payments you make during the repayment period will be applied to the interest first, then the principal of your loan.

Online banking

  • add the National Student Loans Service Centre as a payee through your online banking site
  • use the borrower's loan number as your 7-digit account number

By mail

Have the NSLSC withdraw a one-time payment from the bank account on your file or listed on the form:

  • One-Time Payment form (PDF, 100 KB)

Fill out the form and mail it to:

National Student Loan Service Centre

Mississauga, ON
L5A 4M4

Online lump sum payments with HigherEdPoints.com

If you have an account with HigherEdPoints.com, a third party program to use reward points on student loans, you can use it to make a lump sum payment.
