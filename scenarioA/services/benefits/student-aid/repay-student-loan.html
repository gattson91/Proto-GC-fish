Repay a student loan - When to start

  • How to repay
  • Repayment assistance

The six months period after leaving school

There is a six-month non-repayment period until you have to begin making payments on your student loans.

Interest will start to accumulate at the beginning of this six-month period.

The six-month non-repayment period starts after you:

  • finish your final school term; or
  • reduce from full-time to part-time studies; or
  • leave school or take time off school

Payments before the six-month period has ended

You can make payments at any time after receiving your loan by making a lump-sum payment. Payments will decrease how much interest accrues.

Payments after the six-month period

Before the six-month non-repayment period ends, you will receive a notice. This notice will tell you that your loan repayments are about to begin. You can then confirm how you will make monthly payments and the date payments will start.

Repayments will begin to be withdrawn automatically from the account in which the loan was deposited, if you are set up for automatic withdrawals.

If you are using another monthly payment method, you will need to make payments each month before your deadline.

After the six-month period, you can also start making lump-sum payments of the interest on your loan. You can make a lump-sum payment at any time.

Still studying or in the Canadian Forces

As long as you're in school or a reservist in the Canadian Forces in a designated operation, you do not have to make payments on Canada Student Loans or provincial student loans.
